Problem: Prove that the fraction $\dfrac{21n + 4}{14n + 3}$ is irreducible for every natural number n. (Source: 1959 IMO)

Solution: According to Bezout's identity, $21n + 4$ and $14n + 3$ are relatively prime if and only if there exist integers $x$ and $y$ such that $(21n + 4)x + (14n + 3)y = 1$.

Let $x = -2$ and $y = 3$.

\begin{align*}
(21n + 4)x + (14n + 3)y &= (21n + 4)(-2) + (14n + 3)(3) \\
&= -42n - 8 + 42n + 9 \\
&= 1
\end{align*}

It follows, from Bezout's identity, that $21n + 4$ and $14n + 3$ are relatively prime.

Thus the fraction $\dfrac{21n + 4}{14n + 3}$ is irreducible for every natural number n.
